  • Purity Of Khadi


    • Khadi is hand spun and hand woven in cotton, although silk, wool and blends can also be used which are spun into threads on a charkha. Khadi is a versatle fabric, during spinning of khadi, the threads are inter woven in such a manner that it provides passage of air circulation in the fabrics khadi's weave create air pockets which mae it cool in summer and warm in winters.

    It is very soothing and has got inherit strength which make it highly durable. However, khadi itself had become diversified by the fineness of its weave. We may conclude that if material culture is the primary focus of a study, then human agency becomes the subject, for people manipulate objects such as clothes to define themselves, and Khadi did exactly this.

  • Eco Friendly Khadi

    • At this time, when a lot of noise is made about ecofriendly stuffs, Khadi is the right solution.

    Khadi is hand spun and hand woven, and doesn’t rely on electrical units and manufacturing processes hence don’t generate any toxic waste products.

    The Khadi is most sustainable and ecofriendlyfabric, it is the only fabric which doesn’t utilize fossil fuel.It has the lowest carbon foot print and Can become completely green in vegetable dyes.

    Did you know - that production of one meter Khadi fabric consumes 3 liter of water whereas a conventional textile mill would needs 55 liter.

  • Khadi as pride of the nation

    • It was back in the 1920s, as India fought for her Independence, that Mahatma Gandhi brought back the process of hand weaving. And khadi become the icon of the swadeshi movement The key to Khadi becoming a successful tool for the freedom struggle lies in its uniqueness to which people from diverse backgrounds could relate to.

    Khadi entailed as a national symbol was due to the reason that everybody could wear the same form of clothing without any distinctions of high and low and thus demonstrate responsibility towards the nation and the struggle going through.
